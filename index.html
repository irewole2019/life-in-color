About Dr. Ngozi Rosemary Akande

"A journey painted with service, soul, and creativity."

Dr. Ngozi Rosemary Akande is a prolific artist who uses her art as an advocacy for women's rights and empowerment. She is a mentor to younger artists, women, and children through art workshops, career talks in schools, and art competitions for boys and girls. She presented an art workshop for children in July 2015 at the World Children Festival at the Eclipse in Washington, U.S., and represented Nigeria at the UNESCO Andorra Art Camp For Peace, coming top in Africa.

She has received several awards, including FRCN Rare Gem as a mentor (2016) and Lagos Ambassador (2017), for sustainability and promotion of arts in Nigeria. One of her paintings, "Life First Food" (Pastel on Paper), was used by WHO as a campaign photo for exclusive breastfeeding in Nigerian Hospitals for a period of 10 years (1992-2002).

A versatile artist who works with acrylic, pastel, mixed media, and oil colors, Dr. Akande has held significant positions in the art community, including Secretary of the Female Artists Association of Nigeria (FEAAN) from 2001-2011, Secretary of the Society of Nigerian Artists (SNA) from 2007-2009, and President of FEAAN from 2011 to March 2018. She is also an art administrator, currently serving as a Director of Arts and Craft at the National Council for Arts and Culture (NCAC), Abuja.
